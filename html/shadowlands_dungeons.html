Shadowlands Dungeons

With the destruction of thw vale that seperated Shadowlands from Azeroth comes an all new adventure. Shadowlands, the home to the souls that have passed from the world of living, offers 5 new zones for adventures to quest through along with 8 new dungeons, 4 leveling and 4 max level dungeons, as well as a new, ever changing dungeon that players can solo or group run through. This dungeon offers unique rewards that players will need to collect to craft legendary weapons and gear.

The Necrotic Wake

As one of the first leveling dungeons players will face, Necrotic Wake offers some unique mob interactions to include necromancers who can keep their dead allies alive to fight. While mobs fill this dungeon, there are simle pathways to get through with minimal killing should you wish to speed run through it for the dungeon experiance points.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Blightbone

As the first boss of Necrotic wake, Blightbone patrols the area just in front of the dungeon entrance.
Play style:
Dodge his frontal cone attack.
Kill worms as fast as possible and run if fixated.
Stay out of green pools spawned by boss.

Amarth

The second boss of Necrotic spawns after all the trash in the area suddenly dies.
Play style:
Interrupt Necrotic Bolt.
Dodge frontal breath attack.
Remove enrage if possible and kill adds when summoned.
Boss will detonate all adds causing AOE damage, focus adds down fast.

Surgeon Stitchflesh

As the third boss of Necrotic, Stitchflesh is immune to damage while on his platform. Start by attacking his minion.
Play style:
Attack and pull minion into center of room.
Dodge green swirls spawned by Embalming Ichor
If trageted by “Meat Hook”, move so that the arrow points toward boss, then dodge at end of cast.
Focus boss when pulled from plaform. Repeat untill boss is dead.

Naithor

Naithor as the final boss of Necrotic is a straight forward boss.
Play style:
Keep spread out is possible and move out of blue circles that spawn.
Dodge blue swirls that appear under feet.
If chosen to be sent down, the player will run to end of platform while dodging swirls, then kill add to return to upper level.

Theater of Pain

The Theater of Pain dungeon is one of the four leveling dungeons in Shadowlands. This dungeon offers unique challenges, including a three way boss fight and a one on one pvp brawl. The majority of this dungeon will consist of the bosses them selves as there are only a few mobs to kill. After killing the trio of bosses to start the dungeon, your free to adventure to any other boss.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

An Affront of Challengers

As the first boss of Theater of Pain, the Affront of Challengers is a trio of fighters, each coming with their own abilities and each gains a buff at 40% health.
Play style:
Kill each boss one at a time to prevent an overlap of buffs on Bosses.
Follow the kill order: Sathel > Paceran > Dessia
Run away when fixated, don't stand in green, dispel Spectral Transference, stand by yourself with disease.

Xav the Unfallen

As one of the secondary bosses, Xav is known for sparing and loves watching players fight it out in the arena.
Play style:
Focus and Kill "Oppressive Banner" when spawned.
Dodge "Crushing Slam" and "Massive Cleave" when Xav gets to full energy.
If sent to duel, the winner gets a buff and the loser gets a debuff for 30 secs.

Gorechop

As another secondary boss, Gorechop is a simple boss to defeat, as long as you follow basic mechanics.
Play style:
Dodge "Meat Hooks" that sweep across floor.
Kill adds that spawn during sweeping meat hooks.
Move out of and don't stand in green pools dropped by adds.

Kul'tharok

As the last secondary boss, there are a few mechanics that strongly need to be followed.
Play style:
Tank must remain with boss at all times or get stacking DOT.
DPS and Healr need to stay slightly spread out to avoid damage from Haunt.
If possible, dispell Haunt as quickly as possible and dodge green swirlies.
If "Draw Soul" is cast on you, run behind the green circle on floor so that it's between you and boss. Recollect soul after stun has warn off.

Mordretha, the Endless Empress

As the final boss of Theator of Pain, Mordretha's fight is simple to complete.
Play style:
Dodge frontal cone attach and run from it's random directional rotation.
Spread out when group gets green circle around feet.
Move to opposite side of room when "Grasping Rift" spawns and dont get pulled in by it.

Mists of Tirna Scithe

Mists of Tirna Scithe is another leveling dungeon that allows you to play games within it... well sort of. Everyone will have to find their way through a puzzle maze during this dungeon. Failure will send you back to the beginning of the maze.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Ingra Maloch

Ingra Maloch will be your first boss encounter. He spawns a large add that must be killed as fast as possible to get damage buff. Use AOE damage when boss and add are near by to get splash damage on boss.
Play style:
Tank needs to ensure add is always facing away from group.
Dodge frontal cone attack from add and interput Maloch when casting "Tears of the Forest".
Do NOT hit Maloch when "Embrace Darkness" is active to avoid self damage.

Mistcaller

The second boss of Mist is the mistcaller. To reach this boss, you must pass through a maze by selecting the correct symbols and going through the portal.
Play style:
Dodge the frontal cone attack "Dodge Ball"
Tank must interrupt "Patty Cake"
Avoid the blue fox when summoned or get 8 sec stun.
Only kill the clone with the different symbol, or take massive group damage.

Tred'ova

The final boss of Mist is a giant worm called Tred-ova. Very simple fight, but requires a lot of dodging.
Play style:
Dodge all blue and green swirlies.
When "Mind Link" is cast on you and a partner, run away to break.
Tank needs to collect adds, all focus adds down.
If targeted by add, run away while others kill it.

Halls of Atonement

As your last leveling dungeon, Halls of atonement features some serious bosses that can easily wipe a group if mechanics aren't followed. Make sure to follow the guide to ensure success and remember, where there is great trial, there is also great reward.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Halkias, the Sin-Stained Goliath

This boss spawns after you have killed 3 Shards of Halkias, but make sure to kill enough mobs to open the fighting area before doing so.
Play style:
Do not stand on tank as tank will get hit by "Crumbling Slam".
Dont not stand on jagged rocks on ground. If targeted, move so that "Glass Shards are away from boss.
Dispel "Sinlight Visions" and dodge "Refracted Sinlight" (rotating laser beam)

Echelon

In order to spawn this boss, the tank will need to open the door at the top of the stairs.
Play style:
Move away from red pools on ground.
Tank needs to gather adds. Kill each one by one as they explode for group damage.
try to kill adds on top of each other as best as possible.
If cursed with "Stone Shattering Leap", move to dead adds so boss destroys them to prevent ressurecting

High Adjudicator Aleez

The High Adjudicator, Aleez, is a shadow wrathe that is simple to kill, but spawna a adds that can easily kill group members.
Play style:
Always interrupt "Volley of Power" and interuppt "Bolt of Power" when possible.
if targeted by add, run it to a red vessel in the area. Player can stop add by looking at it.
If not targeted by add, stay away to avoid taking damage.
Dodge big red swirlies on ground.

Lord Chamberlain

A vampire lord, Chamberlain has a number of AOE abilities that healers need to heals the group up from.
Play style:
Dodge statues that the boss throws, pushes and pulls.
Dodge the frontal cone attack cast in random direction.
Tank needs to absorb "Ritual of Woe" by standing between boss and red glowing statue.
"Stigma of Pride" does massive damage to tank, focus healing on tank during this DOT.

Spires of Ascension

Spires of Ascension, also know as the heaven dungeon, is one of the four max level dungeons that drop good gear and has some unque bosses. Over all, this dungeon is simple to beat, but mechanics need to be followed.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Kin-Tara

The first boss of Spires is Kin-Tara, a survival hunter that utilizes her pet to attach and wings to fly. You will need to clear all mobs around her to prevent them aggroing during the fight.
Play style:
Tank needs to face her away fron group to prevent AOE damage from "Sweeping Strike".
DPS and Heals need to dodge "Attenuated Barrage" from pet.
Interrupt "Dark Lance" when ever possible to avoid DOT and move away fron "Charged Spear".
Both Kin-Tara and her pet need to die at same time to avoid boss bonus damage ability.

Ventunax

The second boss in Spires is Ventunax. This boss has multiple AOE attacks tht need to be dodged.
Play style:
Tank needs to face boss away fron group and dodge front cone attack.
Dodge dark swirlies on ground and dark bolts that come out of them.
Boss will summon more dark bolts during "Reabsorb Anima".

Oryphrion

Oryphrion is a large robot boss within Spires of Ascension dungeon. This boss requires that he recharge his batteries, which during that time, damage is doubled. So, save cooldowns for this phase.
Play style:
Stay at least 8 yards apart to prevent Group AOE damage.
Dispel tank when hit with "Charged Stomp" and move away from boss.
When you get blue circle around feet, move far from boss to drop, run away after.
Keep boss out of blue circles. When blue circles turn to orbs, players need to soak them.

Devos, Paragon of Doubt

The final boss in Spires is Devos, a winged guardian. This boss should be tanked near the edge of platform.
Play style:
Everyone needs to dodge "Run Through".
Run away from dark orb spawned by boss.
Dispel "Lingering Doubt" and stay out of pool it drops.
When boss flys in air, collect blue orbs and run them to center statue. Use extra action button to shoot boss down.

Plaguefall

Plaquefall, another max level dungeon, offers some fun mechanics with lots of DOTs that can easily kill a group if not properly managed. With many slime mobs and creatures, this dungeon will surely be fun and may bring back some old memories from Vanilla and Burning Crusade encounters.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Globgrog

As the first boss of Plaguefall, this giant green ooze monster has plenty of tricks to kill the group. The area near the boss should be fully cleared before pulling to prevent extra mobs from joining in.
Play style:
When slime adds spawn, tank boss on opposite side when stunning and killing adds.
Stay near the boss when possible and dodge "Slime Wave" frontal cone attack.
Don't get knocked into slime during "Plaguestomp".
Dispel "Debilitating Plague" as soon as possible.

Doctor Ickus

The second boss of Plaguefall, Doctor Ickus, is more like a frog that jumps from platform to platform. You will need to kill all the mobs on the platforms before pulling boss.
Play style:
Dispel "Slime Injection" from tank and kill adds that spawn.
Always dodge "Slime Lunge".
When boss jumps platforms, follow and kill bomb add asap. Bomb will kill group if it goes off.
Kill red blobs, keep boss out of red pool, but group needs to stand in it gain a haste boost.

Domina Venomblade

The third boss of Plaguefall is a large assassin spider that has several spider friends that need to be killed, otherwise, they start attack the group.
Play style:
Dispel or remove "Cytotoxic Slash" from tank.
If you get "Shadow Ambush", a large purple circle around feet, run away from group.
When webs spawn, players need to run to different webs to pull stealthed add. Run them to tank and AOE down.

Margrave Stradama

Margrave is the final boss of Plaguefall and is a water creature that spawns fromt he pool in the center of the room. Everyone needs to stay out of pool to prevent damage.
Play style:
Kill adds spawned by boss near the center pool, tank needs to soak pool dropped by add to prevent group wide damage.
Dodge the tenticles that spawn from the center pool by running to the gaps.

De Other Side

As one of the max level dungeons, De other Side has some serious fun encounters, to include yet another fight with the infamous Millhouse Manstorm, and of course, his girl Millificent. The first three bosses can be fought in any other.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Hakkar the Soulflayer

As one of the first bosses in De Other Side, Hakkar, a giant serpant creature, provides a strong fight that gets worse as the fight goes one, so kill him fast.
Play style:
Run from group if you get "Corrupted Blood", a red circle around feet.
Kill adds as fast as possible and avoid red pools that drop.
At full energy, Hakkar will gain a shield. Break shield and interupt boss before cast goes off.

Manastorms

The infamous couple, the Manastorms, are a tag team boss in which only one is attackable at a time and each have different abilities.
Play style:
Start by attacking Millhouse as Millificent will jump to her platform.
Interrupt Frostbolt and intercept purple beams to gain buff, but only hold for 15 secs max.
Millhouse and Millificent will swap places every 30 secs.
When bombs are dropped, dps need to run to bombs to defuse, otherwise group takes massive damage.

Dealer Xy'exa

Dealer Xy'exa is one of the first three bosses within De Othe Side and this boss has some unique mechanics that require all members to follow otherwise, it'll be an easy wipe.
Play style:
Avoid white lazers while not stepping into swirly white traps.
If targeted with bomb, jump onto swirly white trap just before bomb goes off to avoid group wide damage.
If boss casts bomb on themself, all members need to jump on white swirly trap near end of timer.

Mueh'zala

As the final boss of De Other Side, this boss is a giant that has serious damaging power. Luckely, when you kill the, they do serious damage to the boss, and the fight ends at 10%
Play style:
BEFORE THE BOSS PULL, decide which TWO dps goes to left portal while others go to right.
Tank needs to always be in melee when boss isn't casting.
Dispel ONE debuff from group when applied and avoid purple circles on ground.
Watch boss and dodge strike when he casts "Master of Death".
When boss casts "Shatter Reality", run to your portal, click, and quickly kill add on other side.

Sanguine Depths

As the last of the max level dungeons, this one offers some more simple to fight bosses that should be good for farming gear. There are mechanics that need to be followed, but all are very simple so long as you are watching the boss.

Dungeon Map

Dungeon Bosses

Kryxis the Voracious

The first boss of Sanguine Depths is a large rock monster named Kryxis that loves to do slams. DPS and Healer need to stay to the back of the boss.
Play style:
DO NOT Tank boss facing the edge as he will do a "Vicious Headbutt" knocking all players backward.
Interrupt "Hungering Drain" and dodge "Juggernaut Rush".
When boss casts "Severing Smash, everyone run from boss, collect blacks that spawn afterward.

Executor Tarvold

The second boss, Tarvold, is your normal everyday executioner. The only eral difference is the amount of damage he does.
Play style:
Stay spread out as much as possible.
If targeted by "Castigate, a large red circle, move it away from all other players.
Kill adds when spawned as fast as possible to reduce pool drop size.

Grand Proctor Beryllia

Grand Proctor is a Grimm Reaper looking boss with only a couple abilities. but those abilities can hit very hard if not dodged.
Play style:
Avoid yellow swirlies as they do large amounts of damage.
When boss casts "Rite of Supremacy", move to yellow circle to avoid blast damage and DOT.

General Kaal

The last boss of Sanguine Depths is Kaal, a gargoyal / vampire mix looking creature with multipl AOE abilities. This boss has a ton of health, but luckily, the fight ends when boss hits 50% HP. Also, one player, preferably the tank or healer, needs to pick up the Naaru relic before pulling the boss.
Play style:
Stay spread out while fighting at least 8 yards to avoid Mass group damage from "Wicked Gash".
Move away from large red laser that spawns and hits a section of platform.
When boss casts "Gloom Squall", play with Naaru relic needs to push extra action button to drop shield. Everyone needs to run to shield to prevent taking massive damage and possibly getting blown off platform.
